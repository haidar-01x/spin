Sustainable Power and Irrigation for Nigeria (SPIN) Project

Nigeria faces a critical challenge in managing its water resources, which is essential for ensuring food security, increasing energy access, and building resilience to climate change. The country's dams and irrigation systems are in disrepair, and over 85% of its hydropower potential remains undeveloped. This lack of investment and fragmented management has left the economy vulnerable to climate shocks like floods and droughts, which have a severe impact on agriculture and livelihoods.

The Sustainable Power and Irrigation for Nigeria (SPIN) Project is designed to address this by strengthening dam safety and improving the management of water resources for hydropower and irrigation. This project aims to rehabilitate critical water infrastructure, boost food production through enhanced irrigation, and increase energy generation. By doing so, it seeks to promote an integrated approach to water management that supports Nigeria’s long-term economic stability and climate resilience.

  • Addressing Nigeria's Water, Food, and Energy Security
  • Water Infrastructure and Dam Management
  • Food Production and Irrigation
  • Power Generation and Hydropower Potential
  • A Unified Approach for a Resilient Future

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the main goal of the SPIN project?

The main goal is to strengthen dam safety and improve water resource management for sustainable power generation and irrigation, thereby boosting food security and economic resilience in Nigeria.

How does the project address climate change?

By rehabilitating water infrastructure, SPIN aims to build resilience against climate shocks like floods and droughts, which have a significant impact on agriculture and livelihoods.

What is the economic impact of the project?

The project is expected to significantly boost food production through enhanced irrigation and increase energy generation, contributing to long-term economic stability and growth.
